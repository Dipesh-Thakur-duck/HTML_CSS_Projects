What is BCA? The Digital Catalyst.

Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA) is a four-year undergraduate degree that bridges the gap between core computer science and business applications. Unlike traditional CS degrees, BCA focuses on the practical implementation of technologies in the corporate world.

Frequentyly Asked Questions

Is BCA math-heavy? expand_more
While BCA includes mathematics in the first few semesters, it is not as intensive as Engineering or CSIT. It focuses more on the logic required for programming rather than abstract theoretical math.
What is the average starting salary for a BCA graduate? expand_more
In the current market (2025-2026), a skilled graduate can expect a entry-level salary ranging from NPR 35,000 to NPR 55,000 per month in Kathmandu's top tech firms.
Can I study BCA after completing +2 in Management? expand_more
Yes! BCA is designed to be inclusive. Students from Science, Management, Humanities, or Education background are all eligible to apply, provided they meet the minimum grade requirements.
